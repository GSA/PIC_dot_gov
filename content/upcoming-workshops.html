Upcoming Workshops

Office of Shared Solutions and Performance Improvement Spring 2018 Workshops

Learn skills, tools, and best practices to help your organization optimize performance, make data-driven decisions, and be successful with transformative management initiatives. These hands-on workshops are FREE and are offered to federal employees only.


Data, Measures, and Insights

April 6, 2018

1:00-4:00 PM (EDT) at GSA, 1800 F Street, NW, Washington, DC 20405, Room 1461

Learn to develop performance indicators that are measurable, relevant to stakeholders, and achievable. This hands-on workshop will provide creative ways to visualize measures in order to tell compelling stories on progress, challenges, and opportunities for initiatives. Attendees will also learn about Data Driven Reviews and how they are used across the government.

Click here to RSVP.


Modernization & Migration Management 101

April 20, 2018

9:00-11:30 AM (EDT) at GSA, 1800 F Street, NW, Washington, DC 20405, Room 1459

Learn about best practices, lessons learned, and tools available to help your agency achieve successful outcomes and reduce risk during administrative system and/or service modernizations and migrations. This workshop will include an interactive activity and provide information on tools available to help agencies transform administrative services through the Modernization and Migration Management (M3) Framework.

Click here to RSVP.

Stakeholder Mapping

May 11, 2018

9:00- 11:30 AM (EDT) at GSA, 1800 F Street, NW, Washington, DC 20405, Room 1460

Learn how to use stakeholder mapping to identify the interactions between your organization and other organizations and individuals. This workshop will share techniques to gain insights into the network of organizations involved with delivering an outcome and the stakeholders associated with specific initiatives.

Strategic Communications

June 29, 2018

9:00- 12:00 AM (EDT) at GSA, 1800 F Street, NW, Washington, DC 20405, Room 1460

Learn how to use proactive and engaging messaging to communicate the intentions, progress, and outcomes of initiatives to stakeholders. This workshop will help attendees gather new ideas, identify unforeseen risks and consequences in communications, and provide instruction on how to drive strategic engagement from a variety of stakeholders.
