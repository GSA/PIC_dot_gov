Chris Polen is an agile leader who believes in getting the job done for the American people. He is a trusted advisor, policy wonk, and special projects coordinator. As the Director of Financial Policy for the Department of Labor, Chris has led formal and informal teams to assess and implement a range of projects. From providing financial policy advice to senior leadership, payment integrity oversight, Departmental and agency-level Enterprise Risk Management, to producing DOL's Agency Financial Report, and supporting agency strategic planning; Chris embraces ambiguity and leads change. Chris won Labor’s “Secretary’s Honor Award” for process improvements and fiscal integrity in 2013, ‘15, and ’17. When not working, Chris is the father of a rambunctious toddler, and pursues a Government Information Leadership M.A. at National Defense University. Prior to federal service, Chris served as an Economic Development Volunteer with the US Peace Corps in Azerbaijan (2008-10) where he supported USAID and regional farmers to improve the agricultural import-export market. In addition, Chris worked in Japan for five years as a teacher, trainer, translator, and consultant.
