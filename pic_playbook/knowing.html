Knowing Practice 1: Data

Capturing qualitative, quantitative, and customer information, using processes to ensure information is valid and accessible

We can easily access or collect the data we need to understand how our program is going. We have a “good-enough” system in place to ensure our data is accurate and valid. The data we collect can be used by others.

Checklist:

  • Organizations use our information to make decisions
  • It is easy for people, with a need to know, to access our performance data
  • We know and can prove that the information is true or correct
  • We know what customer’s experience when they consume our products or services

Knowing Practice 2: Analysis

Generating new or rigorous insights by making sense of data, and applying those insights to performance issues

We connect relevant data together to identify trends, performance issues, or progress made. There are people in our organization who have the necessary skills to analyze data to deepen our understanding, answer key questions and inform decision-making. We regularly use customer feedback data to create greater awareness or understanding about our performance.

Checklist:

  • We are able to identify trends that reflect or impact our performance
  • We are able to pinpoint specific issues or opportunities to improve
  • We have the skills needed to understand the information we have available to us
  • There are standards or processes that we use to routinely make sense of data
  • We benchmark against our past performance, other comparable groups, or across geographic regions to understand how one result is both similar to and distinct from others

Knowing Practice 3: Measurement

Creating a common understanding of data and what needs to be measured, at various stages of achievement

We are able to capture final results, even if it requires using multiple sources of information. Our definitions of “success” reflect multiple stakeholder perspectives. The measures we choose and the targets we set are meaningful – they make sense to people on our team and are clearly connected to our goals. The measures we choose may incorporate many different contributions, but we are able to understand our contribution towards goal achievement.

Checklist:

  • We include people external to our organization or team when determining what to measure
  • The definitions of success that we use reflect multiple stakeholder perspectives
  • People think that our measures are useful
  • We revise our measures over time
  • We are using measures that capture final outcomes in addition to our activities and outputs
